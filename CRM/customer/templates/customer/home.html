RFM Analysis

RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis is a marketing technique used to segment customers based on their transaction history.

  • Recency: How recently a customer has made a purchase.
  • Frequency: How often a customer makes purchases.
  • Monetary Value: The total amount of money spent by a customer on purchases.

RFM analysis helps in customer retention by identifying loyal customers and in target marketing by tailoring marketing strategies to different customer segments.

After Customer Segmentation, Companies Can:

Personalized Marketing Campaigns

Targeted Offers: Offer personalized deals for different customer segments. For example, offer discounts to high-frequency buyers to reward their loyalty or special promotions to low-frequency, high-value customers to encourage more frequent purchases.

Tailored Content: Create content that resonates with each segment's preferences. For example, send product recommendations based on previous purchase history or targeted email campaigns addressing specific needs.

Customer Retention Strategies

Engagement Campaigns: Design campaigns to re-engage customers who have not interacted with the store or brand for a while. Offer incentives to bring back customers who are at risk of churning.

Feedback and Improvement: Collect feedback from different customer segments to understand their pain points and improve your product or service offerings accordingly.

Resource Allocation

Focused Budgeting: Allocate marketing budgets more efficiently by focusing on high-value segments that promise better returns on investment.

Strategic Investment: Invest more in retaining high-value customers and converting high-potential leads rather than spreading resources thinly across all segments.
